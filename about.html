What is the significance of Shiva's 3rd eye?

Shiva's third eye represents spiritual wisdom and insight. It symbolizes his ability to see beyond the ordinary and into the essence of things, perceiving truth and knowledge that go beyond the physical realm. The third eye is also said to be the source of Shiva's immense power, and when opened, it can destroy ignorance and evil.

What does the snake around Shiva's neck signify?

The snake around Shiva's neck symbolizes his mastery over fear and death. In Hindu mythology, the snake (often depicted as Vasuki) represents the cyclical nature of time and life (birth, death, and rebirth). By wearing the snake, Shiva shows that he is beyond the limitations of time and mortality, demonstrating his control over both life and death.

Why does Shiva often have a crescent moon in his hair?

The crescent moon in Shiva's hair represents the cyclical nature of time, especially as it relates to the phases of the moon. It also signifies that Shiva has control over time and its endless cycles. Additionally, the moon symbolizes rejuvenation, immortality, and the ability to transcend time, as Shiva is the eternal being who oversees the cosmic cycles.
